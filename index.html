Why Nicosia?

Nicosia, the last divided capital in Europe

Nicosia, the vibrant capital of Cyprus, is a city of fascinating contrasts where ancient history meets modern living. As the last divided capital in Europe, it offers a unique blend of cultures, with its southern Greek Cypriot and northern Turkish Cypriot districts separated by the "Green Line." Visitors can explore the charming Old Town, surrounded by Venetian walls, where cobblestone streets lead to bustling markets, historic churches, and Ottoman-era mosques. Not to be missed is the Leventis Municipal Museum, which showcases the city’s rich history, and the Ledra Street Observatory, offering panoramic views of the city and its divided landscape.

For those seeking a more contemporary experience, Nicosia boasts a thriving café culture, vibrant nightlife, and a burgeoning arts scene. The Cyprus Museum, housing an incredible collection of ancient artifacts, is a must-visit for history enthusiasts. Gourmands can savor local delicacies like halloumi, souvlaki, and loukoumades at traditional tavernas, while shoppers can explore high-end boutiques and quaint artisan shops. With its mix of old-world charm and cosmopolitan flair, Nicosia promises travelers a truly unforgettable experience.

Cafés

My favorite cafés in Nicosia

Giagia Victoria

Right next to the Ledras barricade and crossing point to the ‘Turkish side of the city’, there’s a little cafe bringing some of the most traditional flavours of Greece to Cyprus, Giagia (Grandma) Victoria. Its beautiful yard is literally right next to the barrels and barbed wire. Fascinating and deeply sad if you are Cypriot or know about Cypriot history.

Address

Prodromou 131 G, Nicosia 2064 Cyprus

What I like about it:

This cafe has a lovely view, great for sitting and watching the passers-by. Fans and shade covers on summer days, and heater units on cold days. Coffee and pastries are good, and the price is right.

Pieto

Situated in the heart of the historic Old Town of Nicosia, this charming café offers a delightful escape for both locals and travelers alike. The moment you step inside, you can’t help but feel as though you’ve been transported to a quaint café in Berlin, filled with character and charm.

Address

Ledras 207, Nicosia 1011, Cyprus

What I like about it:

This delightful cafe offers an inviting atmosphere adorned with unique retro decor, making it a cozy haven on Ledra St. Don't miss out on the charming ambiance, melodic music, and even friendly cats that enhance your visit!

Caffé Nero Eleftheria Square

With the completion of Eleftheria Square ushering in a new era of revitalisation, Caffé Nero is a welcome chain and will surely be successful. This new café is set in a peaceful and comfortable environment, ideal for relaxation.Initial feedback since the opening has been overwhelmingly positive, with a significant increase in visitor numbers.

Address

Eleftheria Square, Konstantinou Palaiologou, Nicosia 1010, Cyprus

What I like about it:

I've always loved walking into a Caffé Nero, with its old-world vibe, leather chairs, nooks to tuck away into, and warm atmosphere. I also enjoy the choice of music, usually.
